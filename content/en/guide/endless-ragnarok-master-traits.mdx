<Section icon={Map} title="Endless Ragnarok Master Traits Progression Guide">

The major expansion introduces a game-changing character specialization system [2]. Once a character reaches Level 100, they can invest Mastery Points (MSP) to raise their Master Level up to a cap of 50 [2]. Raising this level awards specialized Style Materials (Circles, Diamonds, Hexagons, and Gold-Framed Hexagons) used to activate unique perks across three distinct branching paths: **Insight**, **Essence**, and **Crux** [2]. 

**Video Highlights:**
- **Accessing the Expansion**: Complete the base game's story up to Proto Bahamut, then head to Seed Hollow to find the portal [1].
- **Conflux Roguelike Mode**: Farm the single-player Conflux arenas to stock up on character upgrade materials [1].
- **Endgame Chaos Quests**: Clear Chaos tier quests to acquire supplementary damage sigils and unlock Master Level caps [1, 2].

<Section icon={Shield} title="Endgame Damage Optimization Tips">

Simply unlocking your master traits is not enough; you must optimize them alongside your sigils to bypass the game's strict combat limits.

### Prioritize Damage Cap
In the endgame, raw attack stats become useless if you are hitting your character's damage ceiling [1]. You must equip sigils and activate master traits that specifically boost your **Damage Cap** [1, 2]. This allows your high attack power to translate into actual damage dealt [1].

### Use the Training Dummy Percentage Feature
The expansion adds a highly useful **damage cap percentage option** to the training dummy [1]. Fast travel to the training room and enable this option to see exactly how close your attacks are to reaching their maximum limits [1]. 
- **Under 100%**: You need to invest more into raw attack stats [1].
- **Exactly 100%**: Your build is perfectly optimized [1].
- **Over 100%**: You are wasting stats on redundant attack power and should swap some sigils for utility or survivability [1].

| Recommended Sigil Type | Primary Combat Benefit |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Fatebreaker** | Pushes your damage cap higher than what was possible in the base game [1]. |
| **Supplementary Damage** | Adds extra hits that bypass the damage cap entirely [1]. |
| **Potion Hoarder** | Increases your inventory of healing items, making Chaos encounters highly forgiving [1]. |
| **Auto Revive** | Automatically recovers your character after being downed to save a failing run [1]. |

<Callout type="info" title="Summon Costs">
End-game EX traits allow you to manipulate your Summon Costs [2]. You can choose to weaken summon specifications to reduce your Summon Cost by 1, or strengthen summon specifications in exchange for raising the cost by 1 [2]. Use this balance to customize your loadout for Chaos difficulty fights [1, 2].
